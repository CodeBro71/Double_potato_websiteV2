Overview

The Great Irish Potato Famine, also known as the Great Hunger, was a period of mass starvation, disease, and emigration in Ireland between 1845 and 1852.

Causes

The primary cause of the famine was a potato disease known as late blight (Phytophthora infestans).

Impact

The famine had profound impacts, including the loss of approximately one million lives and another million emigrating from Ireland.
